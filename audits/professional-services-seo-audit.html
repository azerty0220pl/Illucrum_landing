Why standard audits miss what matters for your sector

If your firm provides legal, financial, or professional advice, Google classifies your site under YMYL: Your Money or Your Life. This means your content is held to a higher quality standard than a typical business website. A generic audit checks your title tags and page speed. It won’t assess whether your author bios demonstrate genuine expertise, whether your content meets YMYL accuracy standards, or whether your accreditations are visible where Google expects to find them.

E-E-A-T: Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness; is the framework Google uses to evaluate your site. For a law firm or accountancy, failing E-E-A-T suppresses your rankings regardless of how clean your technical SEO is. This audit puts E-E-A-T at the centre.

What is E-E-A-T in SEO?

E-E-A-T stands for Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. It is a framework Google uses to assess content quality, especially on YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) sites, those covering health, finance, or legal topics. For professional services firms, demonstrating E-E-A-T through named authors, visible credentials, and authoritative content is a direct ranking factor.

The Professional Services SEO Audit assesses E-E-A-T through multiple dedicated checks: author bios and bylines, team credential visibility, accreditation display, regulatory body presence, case study quality, and YMYL content standards. These are the signals Google’s quality raters are explicitly trained to look for.
