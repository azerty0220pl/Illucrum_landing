Why generic SEO advice fails SaaS

Organic traffic is up. Trial signups aren’t.

Most SaaS sites we audit have at least one of these issues, and the team usually doesn’t know about it. The blog ranks but trial signups don’t move. The pricing page is the highest-intent page on the site and has no internal links pointing at it. Half the homepage content is rendered in JavaScript that Google sees as an empty shell. Comparison pages (“your product vs competitor”) are the highest-converting organic queries in the entire category, and you’re ranking on none of them.

A generic SEO audit will not flag any of this. It will tell you to write more blog posts and fix some title tags. This audit is built around the SaaS funnel: it checks the structures that turn organic traffic into trials and demos, and the technical layers that decide whether Google can see your product at all.

Built for: B2B SaaS, B2C SaaS, software-as-a-service products, freemium and trial-based platforms, demo-driven tools.

What the Audit Covers

4

Trial / Demo Page Audit

5

Programmatic SEO Potential Full only

7

Content Gap & Blog Strategy Full only

Competitor & SERP Analysis

Top 3 organic competitors, authority comparison, G2 / Capterra / Product Hunt profile consistency, content benchmarking, SERP feature opportunities. 3 Essential + 2 Full.

Backlink Profile Full only

Referring domain count and growth, anchor text distribution, competitor link gap. 3 Full checks.

10

UX & Conversion (SaaS-Adapted)

Signup / trial flow friction, page layout and readability, dwell-time risk, accessibility, overall conversion funnel coherence. 4 Essential + 1 Full.
